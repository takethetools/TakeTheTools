### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is URL Encoding?** The process of turning reserved or non-ASCII characters into a safe format (Percent-Encoding) that web browsers and servers can correctly interpret.
2. **Why do I need to encode spaces?** Spaces are not allowed in URLs; they must be encoded as `%20` or sometimes `+` to prevent the browser from breaking the link.
3. **What is "Percent-Encoding"?** The official name for the system where characters are replaced by a `%` followed by their two-digit hexadecimal value.
4. **Does it encode the entire URL?** Usually, you should only encode the "values" of your query parameters, not the protocol (`https://`) or domain.
5. **How do I encode emojis?** Simply paste them into our tool; we use UTF-8 multi-byte encoding to ensure they are URL-safe.
6. **What is the difference between URL and URI?** A URI is the general category; a URL is a specific type of URI that provides a location for a resource.
7. **Can I decode back?** Yes, use our companion URL Decoder tool for a premium data-reversal experience.
8. **How precise is our encoder?** We use the industry-standard `encodeURIComponent` logic to ensure your strings are compatible with 100% of modern web technologies.
9. **Is encoding good for SEO?** Yes, properly encoded URLs are easier for search engine bots to crawl and index without getting "trapped" or redirected.
10. **Is my data safe?** Yes, our tool works entirely offline in your browser; your sensitive query data never leaves your computer.

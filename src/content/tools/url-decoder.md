### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is URL Decoding?** The process of turning percent-encoded characters (like `%20`) back into their original text form (like a space).
2. **Why do URLs use `%20`?** Because the standard for URLs doesn't allow spaces; they must be encoded so the server can receive them correctly.
3. **Can I decode an entire link?** Yes, but the most useful part to decode is the "Query" section where the data values are stored.
4. **How do I handle international characters?** Paste the encoded string into our tool; we use UTF-8 reconstruction to handle É, ñ, and non-latin symbols perfectly.
5. **What is "Double Encoding"?** This happens when a percent symbol itself is encoded as `%25`, resulting in strings like `%2520`.
6. **Is it the same as Base64?** No, Base64 is a different encoding system used for binary data; URL encoding is specifically for making strings safe for URIs.
7. **Can I encode back?** Yes, use our companion URL Encoder tool for a premium data-reversal experience.
8. **How precise is our decoder?** We use the industry-standard `decodeURIComponent` logic to ensure your strings are compatible with 100% of modern web technologies.
9. **Is decoding safe for passwords?** Generally yes, but you should only decode them in a secure, local environment like our tool.
10. **Is my data safe?** Yes, our tool works entirely offline in your browser; your sensitive encoded data never leaves your computer.

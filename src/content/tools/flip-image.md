## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Axis Mirroring and Matrices

To understand how an image flips, we must first look at the **Linear Algebra of the Pixel Grid**.

### 1.1 The Horizontal Flip (Matrix Reflection)
Flipping an image horizontally means transforming every pixel coordinate $(x, y)$ into $(Width - x, y)$.
- **The Transformation Matrix**:
  `[ -1  0 ] [ x ]`
  `[  0  1 ] [ y ]`
This "Negative Identity" operation effectively mirrors the image across the vertical Y-axis. Our engine performs this mapping in constant O(1) space per pixel, ensuring zero-latency transformation even for 4K assets.

### 1.2 The Vertical Flip (The "Water" Reflection)
Flipping vertically transforms $(x, y)$ into $(x, Height - y)$.
- **Technical Note**: In 20/26, vertical flipping is widely used in "Water Reflection" simulations in game engines and high-end digital art. By mirroring the top half of a composition, designers create a "Natural Symmetry" that builds immediate visual trust.

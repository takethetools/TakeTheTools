### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **How do I convert Newton-meters to Foot-pounds?** Multiply the N⋅m value by 0.7375.
2. **What is the difference between lb⋅ft and ft⋅lb?** While often used interchangeably, "lb⋅ft" is the correct scientific term for torque, while "ft⋅lb" is the term for energy/work.
3. **Why do car engines use foot-pounds but motors use Newton-meters?** It is a byproduct of regional engineering traditions (US vs. Europe/Asia).
4. **What is "Breakaway Torque"?** It is the force required to start a stationary object rotating.
5. **How does a torque wrench work?** It uses a calibrated internal spring or electronic sensor to measure the force applied to a fastener.
6. **Is 1 N⋅m a lot of force?** It is roughly the torque required to tighten a soda bottle cap.
7. **What is a "Newton"?** It is the amount of force required to accelerate 1kg at 1 m/s^2.
8. **How precise is our converter?** We use high-precision floating-point arithmetic suitable for most industrial and mechanical applications.
9. **What is "Backlash" in torque?** It is the clearance or "play" in a mechanical system that can affect torque measurement.
10. **Can torque be negative?** Yes, it simply indicates rotation in the opposite direction (clockwise vs. counter-clockwise).

### A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Content Formats

Before the "Markdown Revolution," authors were forced to use heavy WYSIWYG editors or write raw HTML, which was distracting and difficult to style consistently. 

- **The HTML Era:** Since the dawn of the web, HTML has been the "One True Language" of browsers, powerful but "noisy" for human authors.
- **The Markdown Era:** In 2004, John Gruber and Aaron Swartz created Markdown to provide a format that was "easy-to-read, easy-to-write," yet could be easily converted back to HTML.
- **The Obsidian/Notion Era:** Today, we use Markdown for everything from personal notes to high-stakes technical manuals, defining "The Source" by the fundamental laws of simplicity. Our tool leverages the results of these developments for your daily work.

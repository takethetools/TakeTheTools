### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **How many milliliters are in a liter?** Exactly 1,000 ml.
2. **What is the difference between a US Gallon and a UK Gallon?** A US Gallon is 3.785 liters, while a UK (Imperial) Gallon is 4.546 liters.
3. **How do I convert Liters to Gallons in my head?** Multiply by 0.26 (or divide by approx. 4).
4. **What is a "Cubic Centimeter" (CC)?** It is equal to exactly 1 milliliter.
5. **How does temperature affect volume?** Liquids expand and contract with heat. Professional volume conversion often requires "Thermal Compensation."
6. **What is a "Barrel" in the oil industry?** A standard oil barrel is exactly 42 US gallons.
7. **Is a Cup a standard unit?** In the US, a legal cup is 240 ml, but in recipes, it is often 236.6 ml (8 fl oz).
8. **How precise is our converter?** We use high-precision floating-point arithmetic suitable for most industrial and scientific applications.
9. **What is the largest unit of volume?** For oceans and planets, we use Cubic Kilometers.
10. **What is a "Peck"?** It is a dry volume unit equal to 2 US dry gallons.

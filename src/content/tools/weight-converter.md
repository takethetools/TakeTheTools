### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is the difference between mass and weight?** Mass is the amount of matter; weight is the force of gravity on that matter. Our converter handles mass units used in everyday trade.
2. **Why are there different types of "Ounces"?** Historical trade routes created the "Troy" system for gold and the "Avoirdupois" system for groceries. 
3. **How many grams are in a pound?** Exactly 453.59237 grams.
4. **Who controls the global weight standards?** The BIPM in France manages the International System of Units.
5. **How does altitude affect weight?** You weigh slightly less at the top of a mountain than at sea level, but your mass remains the same.
6. **What is a "Stone"?** It is a common UK unit equal to 14 pounds.
7. **Is a Metric Ton the same as a US Ton?** No, a Metric Ton is 1,000 kg (approx. 2,204 lbs), while a US Ton is 2,000 lbs.
8. **How do I convert Kilograms to Pounds in my head?** Double the KG and add 10% (e.g., 10kg -> 20 + 2 = 22 lbs).
9. **What is the "Grain" unit?** It is a very small unit based on the weight of a grain of wheat, often used in forensics and reloading.

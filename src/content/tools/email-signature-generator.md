## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Table-Driven Rendering

To understand why email signatures are built differently, we must first look at the **Legacy of Email Rendering Engines**.

### 1.1 Why Modern CSS Fails in the Inbox
While the web has moved to Flexbox and CSS Grid, many email clients (especially Microsoft Outlook) still use the **Word HTML Rendering Engine**.
- **The Conflict**: Word does not understand `display: flex` or even modern `padding`. 
- **The Solution**: Professional signatures are built using `<table>` structures. By using `align="left"`, we simulate floating columns. By using `cellpadding="0"`, we control the spacing with 100% cross-client precision.

### 1.2 The "Box Model" in Email
- **Width Management**: Most signatures are capped at 400px to 600px width. This ensures they don't "Break" the mobile view of the email chain.
- **The Multiplier**: Our generator uses inline `style` attributes on every single `<td>` and `<span>`, ensuring that your color and font choices are "Locked In" and cannot be overridden by the recipient's default inbox settings.

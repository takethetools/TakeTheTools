## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Canvas Rendering and Coordinates

To understand how text is added to an image, we must first look at the **HTML5 Canvas API** and the concept of the "Raster Composition."

### 1.1 The Coordinate System (X, Y)
A digital image is a grid of pixels. When you add text, you are defining a "Destination Coordinate" (e.g., `x: 100, y: 200`).
- **The Anchor Point**: Professional tools allow you to choose "Alignment Anchors"—such as Top-Left, Center, or Bottom-Right. The mathematical calculations for a "Centered Headline" involves: `(Image Width - Text Width) / 2`.
- **The Baseline Logic**: Typography is not placed "On a Line"; it is rendered relative to a "Baseline," which accounts for "Descenders" (the bottom parts of letters like 'g' or 'p').

### 1.2 Alpha Blending and Pixel Overlays
When text is placed over pixels, the computer must calculate how the text color "Blends" with the background.
- **The Math**: `Final Pixel = (Text Color * Opacity) + (Background Color * (1 - Opacity))`.
This "Linear Interpolation" ensures that if you are creating a semi-transparent watermark, the underlying photographic details remain visible through the text.

## 2. A Deep Dive into Digital Typography: Clarity and Sharpness

How does our tool maintain the "Visual Signature" of your brand? It uses advanced **Font Rendering Algorithms**.

### 2.1 Anti-Aliasing and Sub-Pixel Rendering
If you simply "Colored" the pixels for a letter, it would look jagged (pixelated). 
- **The Optimization**: Professional canvas rendering uses **Anti-Aliasing**—adding soft, translucent gray pixels around the edges of black letters to "Fool" the human eye into seeing a smooth curve. This is the hallmark of elite visual engineering.

### 2.2 Kerning and Tracking
- **Kerning**: The specific spacing between individual letter pairs (e.g., 'V' and 'A' need to be closer together than 'M' and 'N').
- **Tracking**: The overall density of the text block.
Our tool utilizes the browser's native Layout Engine to respect these "Typographic Rules," ensuring your "Strategic Technical Assets" look as professional as a high-end magazine layout.

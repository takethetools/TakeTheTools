### A Deep Dive into the Evolution of XML

Before the "XML Era," data was often exchanged in proprietary binary formats or rigid fixed-width text files, making cross-platform communication nearly impossible. 

- **The SGML Era:** The ancestor of XML, originally used for complex documentation systems in the military and aerospace industries.
- **The Web 2.0 Era:** The rise of AJAX and SOAP web services made XML the "Universal Data Language," powering the first wave of real-time web applications.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is XML?** Extensible Markup Language, a format used to store and transport structured data.
2. **Why should I format XML?** Minified XML is nearly impossible for humans to audit during complex system migrations or debugging.
3. **What is a "Tag"?** The basic building block of XML, usually in pairs like `<name>...</name>`.
4. **Does XML need a Schema?** It depends; XML is "Well-Formed" if it follows basic syntax rules, but it needs an XSD (Schema) to be "Valid" against specific rules.
5. **How do I fix a "Parsing Error"?** Paste your code into our tool; if the structure is broken (like a missing closing tag), our formatter will highlight where the hierarchy fails.
6. **Is XML dead?** Far from it! It's the backbone of corporate finance, Microsoft Office, and government data standards worldwide.
7. **What is a "Namespace"?** A way to avoid tag-name conflicts by assigning a unique URI to a group of tags.
8. **How precise is our formatter?** We use the official W3C parsing rules to ensure your attributes and nested elements are perfectly aligned.
9. **Can I convert XML to JSON?** Yes, use our companion XML-to-JSON tool for a premium data-translation experience.
10. **Is my XML safe?** Yes, our tool works entirely offline in your browser; your sensitive enterprise data stays local.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **How do I convert Degrees to Radians?** Multiply the degree value by Pi and divide by 180.
2. **What is a "Radian"?** It is the angle created when the arc length is equal to the radius of the circle.
3. **Why are there 360 degrees in a circle?** It originates from ancient Sumerian astronomy and the ease of dividing 360 into many whole numbers.
4. **What is a "Gradian"?** It is a metric unit of angle where a circle is 400 parts (a right angle is 100).
5. **How does a compass measure angles?** It measures "Magnetic Bearing" relative to the Earth's magnetic north pole.
6. **Is a "Minute of Arc" the same as a time minute?** No, but they share the same base-60 logic. There are 60 arcminutes in one degree.
7. **What is the most precise tool for measuring angles?** The Theodolite or a modern Total Station.
8. **How precise is our converter?** We use high-precision floating-point arithmetic suitable for most engineering and scientific applications.
9. **What is "Parallax"?** The difference in the apparent position of an object viewed along two different lines of sight, measured as an angle.
10. **How do I convert Radians back to Degrees in my head?** Multiply by 57.3 (approx. 180/Pi).

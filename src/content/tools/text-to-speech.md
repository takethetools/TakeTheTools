### A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Speech Synthesis

Before the "Neural TTS Era," computer voices were robotic, monotonous, and often difficult to understand for extended periods because they lacked the natural "Prosody" of a human speaker. 

- **The Concatenative Era:** In the late 90s, voices were created by stitching together tiny snippets of recorded human speech, which often sounded disjointed and "mechanical."
- **The Formant Era:** Early systems used mathematical models of the human vocal tract to generate sounds, making them lightweight but very unnatural.
- **The AI/Neural Era:** Today, we use "Deep Learning" to predict the exact waveform of a professional speaker, defining "The Voice" by the fundamental laws of neural logic. Our tool leverages the results of these developments for your daily work.

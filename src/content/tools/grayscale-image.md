## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: The Luma Formula

To understand how color becomes gray, we must first look at the **Weighted Luminance Equation**.

### 1.1 Why the "Average" (R+G+B)/3 is Wrong
A simple average of Red, Green, and Blue does not account for how humans see.
- **The Perception**: To the human eye, Green is much "Brighter" than Blue. A pure blue light looks dim, while a pure green light looks blinding.
- **The Logic**: To create a "Natural" grayscale image, we must weight the colors differently.

### 1.2 The ITU-R Recommendation BT.709
The global standard for high-definition digital signals uses the following weights:
$Y' = 0.2126 \times R + 0.7152 \times G + 0.0722 \times B$
- **The Result**: By prioritizing Green ($71\%$) and deprioritizing Blue ($7\%$), the gray result matches the "Light Intensity" perceived by the human brain. Our engine performs this "Sensory-Correct" calculation for every pixel with zero-latency.

## 2. A Deep Dive into Gamma Correction and Linearity

Why do some grayscaled images look "Dark and Muddy"? It's because of **Gamma**.

### 2.1 The Logarithmic Nature of the Human Eye
Humans don't see light linearly. We are much more sensitive to changes in dark shadows than in bright highlights.
- **The Conflict**: Digital cameras store light in a "Power Law" (Gamma 2.2). 
- **The Strategy**: Our tool performs **Linear De-gamma** before applying the luminance math, and then reapplies the Gamma curve. This "Scientific Loop" ensures that your "Strategic Visual Assets" maintain their depth and contrast without "Crushing the Blacks."

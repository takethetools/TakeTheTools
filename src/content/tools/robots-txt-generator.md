Before the "Robots Exclusion Protocol," search engines were like unguided machines that would crawl every link they found, often bringing down small servers by hitting too many pages at once.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is a robots.txt file?** A text file at the root of a website that tells search engine crawlers which pages or folders they can or cannot request from your site.
2. **Where does it live?** It must always be in the root directory: `example.com/robots.txt`.
3. **Is it a law?** No, it's a set of "Directives." Reputable bots (Google, Bing) follow it, but malicious bots might ignore it.
4. **Does it remove my site from Google?** No, it only stops the "Crawling." To remove a page that is already indexed, use a `noindex` meta tag.
5. **What is a "Crawl Delay"?** A directive that tells bots to wait a few seconds between requests. Note: Googlebot ignores this; it uses its own speed algorithms.
6. **Can I block AI bots?** Yes, you can target `GPTBot` or `CCBot` specifically to protect your content from AI training.
7. **Is it case-sensitive?** Yes, everything in the path is case-sensitive.
8. **How precise is our generator?** We follow the official 2019 Google Robots.txt specification to ensure your file is 100% valid.
9. **Is it good for SEO?** Extremely! It preserves crawl budget for your most important pages.
10. **Is my data safe?** Yes, our tool works entirely offline in your browser; your sensitive site paths never leave your computer.

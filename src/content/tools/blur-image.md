## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Understanding Convolution

To understand how an image blurs, we must first look at the **Mathematics of the Convolution Matrix**.

### 1.1 The Kernel Logic
In digital image processing, a "Blur" is achieved by moving a small matrix (a kernel) over every pixel in the image.
- **The Calculation**: For each pixel, the software looks at its neighbors, multiplies their colors by the values in the kernel, and calculates a new weighted average. 
- **The Box Blur**: The simplest method, where every neighbor has an equal weight. This is fast but can look "Blocky."

### 1.2 The Gaussian Distribution (The Bell Curve)
Professional-grade blurring uses the **Gaussian Function**. 
- **Technically**: Instead of a flat average, pixels closer to the center have a higher weight than those on the edges. This creates a "Natural Smoothing" effect that mimics the way a physical camera lens goes out of focus.
- **Sigma (σ)**: This variable represents the "Radius" of the blur. A higher Sigma means a wider bell curve, resulting in a more intense and smooth obfuscation.

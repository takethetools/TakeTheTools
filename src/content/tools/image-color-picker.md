### A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Digital Palettes

Before the "Eyedropper Tool," designers had to rely on printed color swatches (like Pantone books) and manual matching, which was slow and prone to error under different lighting conditions.

- **The TrueColor Era:** The rise of 24-bit and 32-bit graphics allowed for millions of colors, making high-precision sampling a technical necessity.

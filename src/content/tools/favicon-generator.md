## What is a Favicon?

A **Favicon** (short for "favorite icon") is a small icon associated with a particular website or web page. They appear in your browser's address bar, next to the page name in a tab, and in the user's bookmarks list. 

Modern favicons have evolved beyond a simple 16x16 pixel icon. Today, they are served as a collection of different sizes and formats to ensure they look sharp on everything from a high-resolution 5K monitor to the home screen of an iPhone. ## Tips and Best Practices for a Great Favicon

-   **Keep it Simple**: A 16x16 pixel space is tiny. Avoid text and complex gradients. Use bold shapes, a single recognizable letter, or a simplified version of your logo.
-   **Use Contrast**: Favicons are often displayed on light gray or dark browser tabs. Ensure your icon has a transparent background or high-contrast colors so it pops on any theme.
-   **Test on Both Modes**: Check how your icon looks on a "Dark Mode" browser versus a "Light Mode" browser. Sometimes adding a subtle white outline can help dark icons stand out on dark tabs.
-   **Stick to Square**: Even if our tool supports other shapes, a square 1:1 ratio is the industry standard. This prevents unwanted stretching or squishing during the scaling process.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1.  **Using Too Much Detail**: Trying to cram a whole company name into a favicon results in a blurry smudge. Use just your brand's icon or first letter.
2.  **Poor Transparency**: If your logo has jagged "white pixels" around the edges, they will show up clearly on a dark browser tab. Use our tool with a clean, transparent PNG for the best result.
3.  **Forgetting Mobile Icons**: Many users save sites to their phone's home screen. If you only provide a 16x16 `.ico` file, it will look terrible as a mobile app icon. Use our "Full Package" to avoid this.

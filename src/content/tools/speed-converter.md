### A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Speed Measurement

Before the "Digital Speedometer," humans measured speed by mechanical or visual relative markers. 

- **The Ancient Era:** Speed was measured in "Days of Travel" or "Stadia per Hour."
- **The Maritime Era:** Sailors developed the "Log Line"—a literal rope with knots tied at specific intervals—to measure how fast the ship pulled the line into the water. This is the origin of the "Knot."
- **The Supersonic Era:** The 20th century introduced the "Mach Number," defined as the ratio of an object's speed to the speed of sound in the surrounding medium. This changed aerospace engineering forever.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is a "Knot"?** It is a unit of speed equal to one nautical mile per hour (1.852 km/h).
2. **How fast is Mach 1?** At sea level and standard temperature, it is approx. 1,225 km/h (761 mph).
3. **How do I convert KPH to MPH in my head?** Multiply by 0.6 (e.g., 100 kph -> 60 mph).
4. **What is the fastest man-made object?** The Parker Solar Probe, reaching speeds of over 600,000 km/h.
5. **How does the speed of sound change with altitude?** As air gets colder and thinner at high altitudes, the speed of sound decreases.
6. **Is "Light Speed" a constant?** Yes, in a vacuum, light travels at approx. 299,792,458 meters per second.
7. **What is the difference between speed and velocity?** Speed is a scalar (how fast); velocity is a vector (how fast in a specific direction).
8. **How precise is our converter?** We use high-precision floating-point arithmetic suitable for most industrial and logistical applications.
9. **Why do sailors use knots?** It provides a direct relationship with Earth's geometry (one minute of latitude per hour).
10. **What is "Ground Speed" vs. "Air Speed"?** Ground speed is relative to the Earth; air speed is relative to the moving air around an aircraft.

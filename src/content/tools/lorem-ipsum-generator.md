### 1.1 The "De Finibus" Origin (45 BC)
Lorem Ipsum is not random gibberish. It is a corrupted version of sections 1.10.32 and 1.10.33 of **"De Finibus Bonorum et Malorum"** (On the Extremes of Good and Evil), a philosophical treatise by the Roman statesman **Marcus Tullius Cicero**.
- **The Core Sentence**: The famous opening, *"Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet..."*, is actually a fragment of *"Neque porro quisquam est qui do-lorem ipsum quia dolor sit amet..."* (Translation: *"Neither is there anyone who loves pain itself since it is pain..."*).

### 1.2 The McClintock Discovery
For centuries, designers used this text without knowing where it came from. In the 1980s, **Richard McClintock**, a Latin professor at Hampden-Sydney College, researched the obscure Latin word *"consectetur"* and traced the text back to Cicero's work. His discovery proved that the "dummy text" used by 16th-century printers was a purposeful choice of high-quality classical prose.

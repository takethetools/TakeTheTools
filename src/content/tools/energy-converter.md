### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **How do I convert Joules to Calories?** Divide the Joule value by 4.184.
2. **What is a "Watt-hour"?** It is the energy consumed by a one-watt load for one hour (3,600 Joules).
3. **Why do food labels use "Calories" instead of Joules?** It is a historical tradition in biology, though many countries now list both.
4. **What is a "BTU"?** British Thermal Unit; the amount of heat needed to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit.
5. **How does energy relate to power?** Energy is the capacity to do work; Power is the rate at which that energy is used (Energy = Power × Time).
6. **Is 1 Joule a lot of energy?** It is roughly the energy required to lift a small apple one meter straight up.
7. **What is "Entropy"?** It is the measure of disorder in a system, representing energy that can no longer be used for work.
8. **How precise is our converter?** We use high-precision floating-point arithmetic suitable for most industrial and scientific applications.
9. **What is an "Erg"?** A small unit of energy in the CGS system (10^-7 Joules).
10. **Can energy be destroyed?** According to the First Law of Thermodynamics, energy can only be transformed, not created or destroyed.

## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Mastering the Alpha Channel

To understand how PNG translates to WebP, we must first master the concept of the **32-Bit Pixel**. A standard transparent image contains four channels: Red, Green, Blue, and **Alpha** (transparency).

### 1.1 The PNG Legacy: DEFLATE Compression
PNG uses the "DEFLATE" algorithm (the same one used in `.zip` files). While excellent for data integrity, it's not optimized for the specific patterns of digital graphics. It treats the alpha channel just like any other data stream, leading to files that are "Mathematically Pure" but "Strategically Heavy."

### 1.2 The WebP Advantage: Holistic Lossless
WebP's lossless mode is a revolution. It uses "Transformations" like color indexing, color cache, and palette grouping to find the most efficient way to describe your graphic. This "Alpha-Mapping" is the secret trick for why a transparent WebP can be significantly smaller than a PNG without losing even a single microscopic bit of detail across the bridge.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **How do I convert MPG to L/100km?** Divide 235.215 by the MPG (US) value.
2. **Why does the UK use a different MPG?** The UK uses the Imperial Gallon (4.54L), while the US uses the US Liquid Gallon (3.78L).
3. **Is L/100km more accurate than MPG?** It is more useful for calculating fuel cost over long distances because it is a direct measure of consumption.
4. **What is the "MPGe" rating for electric cars?** It is Miles per Gallon Equivalent, used to compare the energy efficiency of electric vehicles to gas vehicles.
5. **How does aerodynamics affect fuel economy?** Drag increases with the square of speed, meaning driving faster disproportionately increases fuel consumption.
6. **What is the most efficient speed to drive?** For most cars, it is between 45 and 55 mph (70-90 km/h).
7. **How precise is our converter?** We use high-precision floating-point arithmetic suitable for most industrial and logistical applications.
8. **Does fuel weight affect consumption?** Yes, carrying a full tank increases vehicle weight and reduces efficiency slightly.
9. **What is "Brake Specific Fuel Consumption" (BSFC)?** A measure of the fuel efficiency of an internal combustion engine that burns fuel and produces rotational power.
10. **How do I convert km/L to L/100km?** Divide 100 by the km/L value.

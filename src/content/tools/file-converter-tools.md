## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Digital Signal Processing (DSP)

To understand how a file converts, we must first look at the **Physics of the Waveform**.

### 1.1 Sampling and the Nyquist-Shannon Theorem
Audio and video are continuous waves. To store them digitally, we must "Sample" them.
- **The Theorem**: To perfectly recreate a signal, you must sample at twice its highest frequency. This is why standard CD audio is 44.1kHz (doubling the 20kHz limit of human hearing).
- **The Task**: During conversion, our engine must "Resample" the data, using high-precision interpolation to ensure no "Aliasing" or "Jitter" occurs in the new format.

### 1.2 Quantization and Bit-Depth math
Bit-depth (16-bit, 24-bit) defines the "Dynamic Range"—the difference between the quietest and loudest sounds. 
- **The Calculation**: $Range (dB) \approx Bit-Depth \times 6$. 
By converting your professional assets using high-fidelity bit-depth preservation, you ensure your "Strategic Audio Assets" maintain their elite studio quality.

## 2. A Deep Dive into Lossy vs. Lossless Codecs

How does our tool shrink a 1GB WAV file to a 5MB MP3? It uses **Psychoacoustic Synthesis**.

### 2.1 The Spectrum of Fidelity
- **Lossless (FLAC, WAV, ALAC)**: These formats store every bit of the original signal. They are the "Golden Record" for archiving.
- **Lossy (MP3, AAC, OPUS)**: These formats use **Perceptual Coding**. They "Discard" frequencies that the human ear can't hear (masking).
- **The Strategy**: Our converter utilizes the latest **FFmpeg-based** logic to ensure that even at lower bitrates, your "Information Throughput" remains clear and usable.

### 2.2 Video Containers vs. Codecs
A common misunderstanding in 20/26 is the difference between an **MP4** and **H.264**.
- **The Container (MP4, MKV)**: The "Box" that holds the video, audio, and subtitle streams.
- **The Codec (H.264, HEVC, AV1)**: The "Algorithm" used to compress the pixels.
Our tool manages the complex "Demuxing" and "Remuxing" required to change the box without losing the signal.

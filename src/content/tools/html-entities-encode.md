### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is an HTML Entity?** A special string of characters used to display reserved characters or symbols that are not present on a standard keyboard.
2. **Why do I need to encode?** To prevent the browser from interpreting characters like `<` as HTML tags, and to ensure special symbols display correctly across all devices.
3. **What is the difference between `&lt;` and `&#60;`?** `&lt;` is a "Named Entity," which is easier for humans to read. `&#60;` is a "Numeric Entity," which works for every possible character in Unicode.
4. **Does it stop XSS?** It is a critical part of a "Defense in Depth" strategy for preventing Cross-Site Scripting.
5. **What characters should I always encode?** At minimum: `<`, `>`, `&`, `"`, and `'`.
6. **Can I encode Emoji?** Yes, while modern browsers handle them well raw, encoding them as numeric entities ensures 100% compatibility in legacy systems.
7. **Is it the same as URL Encoding?** No, HTML encoding is for content inside a page; URL encoding is for the addresses in the browser bar.
8. **How precise is our encoder?** We follow the official W3C HTML5 named character reference list to ensure your results are 100% valid.
9. **Is encoding good for SEO?** Yes, it prevents "Parsing Errors" that can lead to broken pages or unreadable content for search engine bots.
10. **Is my data safe?** Yes, our tool works entirely offline in your browser; your sensitive proprietary content never leaves your computer.

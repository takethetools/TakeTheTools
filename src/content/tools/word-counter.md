## 2. A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Writing Metrics

Before the "Digital Word Processor," writers used physical and mechanical methods to estimate the length of their work.

### 2.1 The Linotype and Typewriter Era
In the early 20th century, journalists and authors calculated their work using "Page Estimates." A standard double-spaced page on a typewriter was roughly 250 words. This led to the "Strategic Technical Sheets" that editors used to plan newspaper layouts long before the birth of the internet.

### 2.2 The Digital Revolution in Lexicography
In the 1980s, the first word processors (like WordStar and early Microsoft Word) introduced real-time counting. ## 8. Historical Anecdotes: The "Telegram" Economy
In the late 19th century, people communicated via "Telegrams," where every word cost money. This led to the birth of "Telegraphese"—a style of writing that maximized impact with minimum words.

### A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Optical Scanning

Before the "Barcode Standard," inventory tracking required manual entry of long serial numbers, leading to massive human error and slow checkout lines that limited the growth of modern retail. 

- **The Bullseye Era:** In the late 40s, the first barcode concepts were circular, but they were difficult to print accurately with the technology of the time.
- **The UPC Era:** In 1974, the first pack of Wrigley's gum was scanned in Ohio using a linear barcode, revolutionizing the global economy and creating the "UPC" (Universal Product Code) standard.
- **The Global GS1 Era:** Today, we use barcodes for everything from "Serialized Vaccines" to "International Air Freight," defining "The Object" by the fundamental laws of optical logic. Our tool leverages the results of these developments for your daily work.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is a Barcode?** A visual representation of data that can be read by a scanner using a series of parallel lines of varying widths and spacings.
2. **What is Code 128?** A high-density linear barcode symbology that can encode all 128 characters of ASCII. It's the standard for shipping and logistics.
3. **What is EAN-13?** The "International Article Number," used primarily in retail worldwide (except for North America, which uses UPC).
4. **How do I print them?** Use a high-quality "Thermal Printer" for long-lasting labels, or a standard inkjet/laser printer for temp use.
5. **Does the color matter?** Yes! Use high-contrast colors (Black bars on a White background) to ensure a 100% scan rate.
6. **Can I put letters in a barcode?** Only in certain formats like Code 128. Formats like UPC or EAN are strictly limited to numbers.
7. **What is a "Check Digit"?** The last number in a retail barcode, calculated using a formula to ensure the rest of the numbers were scanned correctly.
8. **How precise is our generator?** We use the industry-standard "JsBarcode" logic to ensure your patterns are exactly compliant with global specifications.
9. **Is Barcoding better than RFID?** Barcodes are much cheaper; RFID is for high-end tracking where line-of-sight isn't possible.
10. **Is my data safe?** Yes, our tool works entirely offline in your browser; your sensitive SKU/product data never leaves your computer.

## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: GCD and the Euclidean Algorithm

To understand how these values are found, we must first look at the **Mechanics of Divisibility**.

### 1.1 The Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)
The GCD is the largest positive integer that divides each of the integers without a remainder.
- **The Euclidean Algorithm**: This is the "Engine" of modern GCD calculation. It states that $GCD(a, b) = GCD(b, a \mod b)$. 
- **The Strategy**: This recursive "Subtraction" loop is extremely fast, making it the bedrock of high-performance technical calculations. Our engine performs this with zero-latency, even for numbers in the billions.

### 1.2 The Least Common Multiple (LCM)
The LCM is the smallest positive integer that is divisible by both $a$ and $b$.
- **The Relationship Formula**: $LCM(a, b) = |a \cdot b| / GCD(a, b)$.
By leveraging the GCD, our tool ensures your LCM results are mathematically perfect and linked through "Logical Unity," providing a "Strategic Layer of Accuracy" for your professional operations.

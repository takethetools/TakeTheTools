### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is a Unix Timestamp?** A system for describing a point in time, defined as the number of seconds that have elapsed since 00:00:00 UTC on January 1, 1970.
2. **What is the Epoch?** The "beginning of time" for computer systems, January 1, 1970.
3. **Why use integers for time?** It makes mathematical calculations (like finding the difference between two dates) extremely fast and error-free for machines.
4. **Is it the same as UTC?** Unix time is based on UTC, but it doesn't account for leap seconds.
5. **How do I get the current timestamp?** Click the "Now" button in our tool or use `Date.now() / 1000` in your code.
6. **Will it work past 2038?** Yes, modern 64-bit systems (and our tool) handle dates well into the billions of years.
7. **What is ISO-8601?** The international standard for representing dates and times (`YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM:SSZ`), which we provide as a conversion option.

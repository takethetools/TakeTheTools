### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **How do I convert Newtons to Pounds?** Multiply the Newton value by 0.2248.
2. **What is a "Dyne"?** It is a metric unit of force (CGS system) equal to 10^-5 Newtons.
3. **Why do we use Newtons instead of Kilograms?** Kilogram is a unit of mass; Newton is a unit of force. Weight is the force of gravity on a mass.
4. **What is "Tensile Strength"?** It is the maximum force a material can withstand before breaking under tension.
5. **How does a load cell work?** It converts physical force into an electrical signal through a strain gauge.
6. **Is 1 Newton a lot of force?** It is roughly the force of a small apple sitting on your hand.
7. **What is a "Kip"?** It stands for "Kilo-pound," equal to 1,000 pounds of force.
8. **How precise is our converter?** We use high-precision floating-point arithmetic suitable for most structural and industrial applications.
9. **Does acceleration affect force?** Yes, as defined by Newton's Second Law (F=ma).
10. **What is the largest force in nature?** The strong nuclear force that holds atomic nuclei together.

## About the Sentiment Analysis Tool

**Sentiment Analysis** (also called opinion mining) is the process of using computational methods to determine whether a piece of text expresses a **positive**, **negative**, or **neutral** emotion. Our free online tool does this instantly — paste any text and get results immediately.

### How Sentiment Analysis Works

Our tool uses a **lexicon-based approach**, which assigns sentiment scores to individual words and phrases, then calculates an overall score for the full text.

**Example:**
- "This product is **amazing** and **easy** to use" → Positive (2 positive words)
- "The service was **terrible** and **slow**" → Negative (2 negative words)
- "The package arrived on Tuesday" → Neutral (no sentiment words)

### Real-World Applications

**Customer Feedback Analysis**
Businesses use sentiment analysis to process thousands of reviews automatically. Instead of reading every review manually, you can classify them into positive/negative/neutral buckets.

**Social Media Monitoring**
Track how people feel about your brand on Twitter, Reddit, and other platforms. A spike in negative sentiment might signal a PR issue before it becomes a crisis.

**Content Quality Check**
Writers use sentiment analysis to ensure their content matches the intended tone. A technical document should be neutral; a product page should be positive.

**Market Research**
Analyze news articles, forum posts, and product reviews to understand public opinion about a topic or competitor.

### Sentiment Score Interpretation

| Score Range | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| +0.5 to +1.0 | Strongly Positive |
| +0.1 to +0.5 | Mildly Positive |
| -0.1 to +0.1 | Neutral |
| -0.5 to -0.1 | Mildly Negative |
| -1.0 to -0.5 | Strongly Negative |

### Limitations to Know

Sentiment analysis has known challenges:
- **Sarcasm** — "Oh great, another bug" reads as positive to a lexicon
- **Context** — "This is sick" can be positive slang or negative feedback
- **Domain-specific language** — Medical or legal text may score differently than expected

For high-stakes applications, always combine automated analysis with human review.

### A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Pressure Measurement

Before the "Digital Manometer," humans measured pressure by the height of liquid columns—mercury or water. 

- **The Torricelli Era:** Evangelista Torricelli invented the mercury barometer, proving that air has weight and creating the "Torr" unit (approx. 1 mmHg).
- **The Pascal Era:** Blaise Pascal's work on fluid mechanics defined the principle that pressure is transmitted equally in all directions, the foundation of all modern hydraulics.
- **The Electronic Era:** Today, we use piezoelectric crystals and MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) to measure pressure with sub-millibar precision. Our tool leverages the results of these technologies for your daily work.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **How do I convert PSI to Bar?** Divide the PSI value by 14.5038.
2. **What is a "Pascal"?** It is one Newton of force applied over one square meter of area.
3. **Why are there different types of "Atmospheres"?** Standard Atmosphere (atm) is 101.325 kPa, while Technical Atmosphere (at) is 98.0665 kPa.
4. **What is "Vapor Pressure"?** It is the pressure exerted by a vapor in thermodynamic equilibrium with its condensed phases.
5. **How does altitude affect air pressure?** Pressure decreases as you go higher because there is less air above you pressing down.
6. **What is a "Torr"?** It is a unit of pressure equal to 1/760 of an atmosphere, often used in vacuum science.
7. **Is 1 Bar a lot of pressure?** It is roughly equivalent to the atmospheric pressure at sea level.
8. **How precise is our converter?** We use high-precision floating-point arithmetic suitable for most industrial and scientific applications.
9. **What is "Hydraulic Head"?** It is a measure of liquid pressure expressed in terms of the height of a liquid column.
10. **Can pressure be negative?** "Negative Pressure" usually refers to a partial vacuum relative to the surrounding atmosphere.

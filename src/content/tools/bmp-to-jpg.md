## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Understanding the BMP Structure

To understand how a BMP becomes a JPG, we must first look at the **Raster Grid Identity** of the source.

### 1.1 Device Independent Bitmaps (DIB)
A BMP (Bitmap) is a "Raw" format. It stores every single pixel's color data (usually 24-bit RGB) as a physical coordinate in a grid.
- **The Calculation**: A 1000x1000 pixel image has 1 million pixels. At 3 bytes per pixel (Red, Green, Blue), the file size is exactly 3 million bytes (3MB), plus a small header. No math is used to save space—it is a literal "Map" of the image.

### 1.2 The "Padding" Protocol
BMP files require every row of pixels to be a multiple of 4 bytes. If it isn't, the software adds "Zero-Padding." Our engine identifies and strips this structural bloat before the transformation, ensuring your "Master Data" is clean for the conversion process.

## 2. A Deep Dive into the JPG Transformation (DCT)

How does our tool shrink a 3MB file to 100KB without looking "Blurry"? It uses **Lossy Synthesis**.

### 2.1 Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT)
While BMP sees pixels, JPG sees "Waves."
- **Technically**: The engine breaks the image into 8x8 pixel blocks. It then performs a DCT to convert the "Spatial Data" into "Frequency Data." 
- **The Filter**: Humans are very sensitive to large areas of color but less sensitive to high-frequency tiny details. JPG "Discards" the frequencies we can't see.

### 2.2 Quantization and Huffman Coding
- **Quantization**: This is the "Decision Layer" where the quality slider lives. It rounds off the high-frequency values to zero.
- **Huffman Coding**: A final layer of lossless compression that packs the remaining data into the smallest possible binary stream.
Our converter utilizes these "High-Performance Protocols" to ensure your resulting assets are as light as possible while maintaining professional visual fidelity.

## 3. The Science of Color-Space Mapping (YCbCr)

To understand why professional conversion is essential, we must look at the "Logic of Human Vision":

#### 3.1 Moving from RGB to YCbCr
BMPs use **RGB** (Red, Green, Blue). Professional JPG converters first transform this into **YCbCr**.
- **Y (Luminance)**: The brightness/detail.
- **Cb & Cr (Chrominance)**: The color data.
Because our eyes care more about "Brightness" than "Color Density," the engine can technically "Compress the Color" more than the "Detail," resulting in more "Strategic Visual Gains" for your file size.

#### 3.2 Stripping the Legacy Bloat
Legacy BMPs often carry unneeded "Color Tables" or "DIB Headers" that modern browsers don't need. Our tool sanitizes the output, creating a "Pure JPG Stream" that is 100% compliant with the latest global web standards.

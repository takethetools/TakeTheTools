## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: The exponential Equation

To understand how wealth grows, we must look at the core formula for **Continuous and Periodic Compounding**.

### 1.1 The Standard Formula
The mathematical representation of compound interest for a periodic schedule is:
`A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)`
Where:
- **A**: The Final Amount (Future Value).
- **P**: The Principal (Initial Investment).
- **r**: The Annual Interest Rate (Decimal).
- **n**: The number of times interest is compounded per year.
- **t**: The time the money is invested for (Years).

### 1.2 The Magic of 'n' (Compounding Frequency)
The more frequently interest is calculated (daily vs. annually), the faster the wealth grows. Even with the same interest rate, "Daily Compounding" results in a higher "Effective Annual Yield" than "Annual Compounding." ## 7. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is Compound Interest?** Earning interest on both your initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods.
2. **How is it different from Simple Interest?** Simple interest only calculates earnings on the original principal, leading to much slower growth.
3. **What is the "Rule of 72"?** A shortcut to estimate doubling time (72 / rate = years).
4. **How does inflation affect my savings?** Inflation reduces the "purchasing power" of your future dollars, even as your total balance increases.
5. **What is APY vs. APR?** APY (Annual Percentage Yield) includes compounding; APR (Annual Percentage Rate) does not.
6. **Can I calculate debt with this?** Yes, credit card interest and many loans use the same compounding logic (but to your disadvantage).

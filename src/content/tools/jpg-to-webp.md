## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: From DCT to Predictions

To understand how JPG translates to WebP, we must first master the concept of the **Transform**. 

### 1.1 The JPG Legacy: Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT)
JPG works by dividing an image into 8x8 blocks and using complex math (DCT) to discard details that the human eye "probably won't miss." This works well for photos, but it creates "Ringing" and "Blockiness" when compressed too far, defining its limits in the "High-Clout" professional world.

### 1.2 The WebP Advantage: Intra-Frame Prediction
WebP takes the 8x8 block idea even further. It doesn't just simplify the block; it looks at the blocks *already rendered* next to it and "Calculates" what the next block should look like. It then only stores the "Error" between the calculation and the real pixels. This "Error-Mapping" is the secret trick for why a WebP file is always lighter than its JPG ancestor.

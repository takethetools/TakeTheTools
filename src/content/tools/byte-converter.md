### A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Binary Storage

Before the "Byte" became the world standard, computer manufacturers experimented with 6-bit, 7-bit, and 9-bit architectures. 

- **The Early Computing Era:** The term "Byte" was coined in 1956 by Werner Buchholz during the design of the IBM Stretch computer. It was originally defined as a group of bits to represent a character.
- **The Standardization Era:** The 8-bit byte became dominant with the IBM System/360, eventually becoming the universal building block of all digital information.
- **The Binary Prefix Era:** The growth of storage led to the creation of Kilobytes (1,000) and Kibibytes (1,024), a distinction that continues to confuse engineers and consumers alike.

  bits: 0.125,

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **How many bits are in a byte?** Exactly 8 bits.
2. **What is a "Kilobyte"?** In most computing contexts, it is 1,024 bytes (also called a Kibibyte).
3. **Why do we use "Bits" for networking but "Bytes" for storage?** Networking is about the signal (pulses); storage is about the character (content).
4. **What is a "Gigabyte"?** 1,024 Megabytes (binary) or 1,000 Megabytes (decimal).
5. **How does compression affect byte size?** It removes redundancy to represent the same information with fewer bytes.
6. **What is the largest unit of data?** Currently, the Yottabyte (10^24 bytes).
7. **Is a Bit the same as a Byte?** No, 1 Byte = 8 Bits. 
8. **How precise is our converter?** We use high-precision floating-point arithmetic suitable for most IT and software engineering applications.
9. **What is a "Word" in computing?** The natural unit of data used by a particular processor design (e.g., 32-bit or 64-bit).
10. **How do I convert bits to bytes in my head?** Divide by 8.

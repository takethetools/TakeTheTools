### A Deep Dive into the Evolution of AES

Before the "Modern Internet," the US government used the Data Encryption Standard (DES), which eventually became easy for supercomputers to break. 

- **The AES Competition:** In 1997, the NIST held a global competition to find a new, unbreakable standard. Joan Daemen and Vincent Rijmen won with their "Rijndael" design, which became AES in 2001.
- **The Silicon Revolution:** The development of AES-NI (New Instructions) in modern CPUs turned encryption into a zero-latency utility, allowing entire hard drives to be encrypted without slowing down the computer.
- **The Modern Privacy Era:** Today, we use AES for "End-to-End Encryption" in apps like WhatsApp and for "Zero-Trust Cloud Storage," defining "The Barrier" by the fundamental laws of mathematical complexity. Our tool leverages the results of these developments for your daily work.

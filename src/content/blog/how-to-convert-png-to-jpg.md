## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Understanding the Conversion Abyss

To understand how PNG translates back to JPG, we must first master the concept of the **Conversion Abyss**—the technical gap between lossless and lossy representations.

### 1.1 The Alpha-Channel Challenge
PNG stands for "Portable Network Graphics" and its defining feature is the "Alpha Channel" (transparency). JPG (Joint Photographic Experts Group) has no concept of transparency. When you cross the bridge, the algorithm must "Flatten" the image, filling in the transparent areas with a solid background (usually white). This "Flattening math" is the most critical part of the conversion process.

### 1.2 The Quantization Shift
PNG uses DEFLATE (Zip-like compression) to keep every pixel. JPG uses "Discrete Cosine Transform" to turn pixels into waves and then "Quantizes" those waves, effectively deleting the parts of the image that the human eye isn't sensitive to. This is why a 10MB PNG can become a 200KB JPG and still look "Professional and Premium" to the casual observer.

## The Hero's Journey of the ASCII String: Turning Text into Pixels

A Base64 string is a "safe" textural representation of binary bits. By taking these characters and re-assembling them into their original byte-aligned structure, we can restore the photographs, diagrams, and icons that make our digital experiences rich and engaging.

## The Technical Deep Dive: The Logic of the Mapping

When you click "Convert" in our tool, our engine performs a high-precision binary expansion:

1. **Header Identification:** We look for the "Magic Bytes" at the start of the string (e.g., `iVBORw` identifies a PNG).
2. **Byte-Aligned Reconstruction:** We take each 4-character Base64 group and expand it into the three 8st-bit binary bytes it represents.
3. **MIME Interpretation:** We use the `data:image/` prefix to tell the browser's GPU exactly how to paint the resulting pixels on your screen.

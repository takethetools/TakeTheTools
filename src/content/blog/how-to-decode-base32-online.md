## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Re-assembling the Bytes

To understand how Base32 translates back, we must first master the concept of the **Bit-Buffer**.

### 1.1 The Mapping to 5-bit Blocks
Each character in a Base32 string represents exactly five bits. The decoding algorithm takes these 5-bit "Pieces" and stacks them into a continuous line of bits. It then "Slices" that line into standard 8-bit pieces to form characters or files.

### 1.2 The Padding Removal
Base32 strings often end with `=` characters. These are indicators for the "Decoding Engine," telling it where the meaningful information ends and the "Technical Padding" begins. Mastering this "Cleanup math" is what ensures your final decoded data is "Proof-Ready" and professional, protecting the sovereignty of your original file across the bridge.

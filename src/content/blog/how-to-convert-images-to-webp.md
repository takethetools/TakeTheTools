## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Understanding the WebP Algorithm

To understand how WebP works, we must first master the concept of **Predictive Encoding**. Unlike JPEG, which simply blocks off parts of an image, WebP uses a more intelligent approach.

### 1.1 Predictive Coding (Intra-Prediction)
WebP looks at neighboring blocks of pixels to predict the values in a new block. It then only encodes the "Residual" (the difference between the prediction and the actual pixel). This "Forecasting math" is what makes WebP significantly more efficient than legacy formats.

### 1.2 Lossy vs. Lossless
One of WebP's greatest strengths is its versatility:
- **WebP Lossy:** Uses the VP8 keyframe coding to compress images. It outperforms JPEG by a significant margin while maintaining high fidelity.
- **WebP Lossless:** Uses a revolutionary algorithm that compresses images without losing a single pixel of data. It results in files that are ~26% smaller than the equivalent PNG.

## 2. A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Web Formats

The journey to the perfect web image began decades ago, but the breakthrough came from an unexpected source.

### 2.1 The Google On2 Breakthrough (2010)
After acquiring On2 Technologies, Google released WebP as an open-source project. It was based on the VP8 video codec, proving that the math used for moving images was actually the most efficient way to store static ones. This "Video-to-Static Bridge" changed the internet forever.

### 2.2 The Browser Support War
For years, WebP adoption was slowed by a lack of support from certain browsers. However, in 2020 and 2021, Apple finally added WebP support to Safari, effectively ending the "Format War" and making WebP the undisputed global standard for modern web performance.

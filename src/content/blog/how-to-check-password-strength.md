## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Entropy and Search Space

To understand how password strength is calculated, we must first master the concept of **Information Entropy**.

### 1.1 The bits of Entropy
Entropy is a measure of randomness. The more "Bits of Entropy" a password has, the harder it is to guess.
- A 4-digit PIN has ~13 bits of entropy.
- A random 12-character alphanumeric password has ~72 bits of entropy.
Every bit of entropy effectively "Doubles" the time it takes to crack your password, defining "Security" by the fundamental laws of exponential growth.

### 1.2 The Search Space Math
The total number of possible combinations (S) is calculated as L^N, where L is the length of the alphabet and N is the length of the password.
- Using only lowercase letters (L=26).
- Adding uppercase letters (L=52).
- Adding numbers and symbols (L=94).
Expanding the "Alphabet Library" is the high-performance design choice that secures your account across the global metrics.

## 2. A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Password Cracking

The journey from "Simple Words" to "Complex Passphrases" has been defined by the ever-increasing power of hardware.

### 2.1 The "RockYou" Era
In the early days of the web, passwords like `123456` and `password` were common. But in 2009, a hack of the "RockYou" social site leaked 32 million passwords, creating a "Dictionary" that hackers still use today to crack weak accounts in milliseconds.

### 2.2 The GPU Revolution
Modern hackers don't use a keyboard to guess your password; they use high-end Graphics Cards (GPUs) that can test billions of combinations per second. This "Hardware Escalation" proved that "Length" is the only true defense against the raw speed of a silicon-based attack.

### 2.3 The zxcvbn Breakthrough
Today, we use the `zxcvbn` library (developed at Dropbox) to check password strength. It doesn't just look for "Numbers and Symbols"; it looks for patterns, dates, and common dictionary words, defining "Strength" by the fundamental laws of human predictability.

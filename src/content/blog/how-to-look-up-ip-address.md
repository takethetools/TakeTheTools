Every device connected to the internet has an IP address that reveals information about its location and network. Our **IP Lookup** tool provides detailed geolocation and network data for any IPv4 or IPv6 address.

## What Information Can You Look Up?

- **Country, Region, City**: Geographic location of the IP.
- **ISP**: Internet Service Provider name.
- **Organization**: The organization that owns the IP block.
- **Timezone**: Local timezone at the IP's location.
- **Coordinates**: Latitude and longitude (approximate).

## How to Look Up an IP Address

1. Open the [IP Lookup Tool](/tools/ip-lookup).
2. Enter any IP address (or leave blank to check your own IP).
3. Click **Look Up** to see all available details.

## Why Look Up an IP?

- **Security**: Identify suspicious logins from unexpected locations.
- **Debugging**: Verify geo-targeting and regional content delivery.
- **Network Admin**: Identify network abuse or spam sources.
- **Rate Limiting**: Block specific IP ranges from your services.

## Limitations of IP Geolocation

IP geolocation is approximate, not precise:
- Accuracy is typically at the **city level**, not street level.
- VPN and proxy users will show the VPN server's location, not their real location.
- Mobile IPs often resolve to a regional hub rather than the actual device location.

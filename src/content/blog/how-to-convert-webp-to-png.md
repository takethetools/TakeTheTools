## 1. The Mathematical Foundation: Decompressing the Prediction

To understand how WebP translates back to PNG, we must first master the concept of **Reverse Mapping**. WebP is an "Intra-frame" predictive format, while PNG is a "Scanline" spatial format.

### 1.1 The WebP Deconstruction: Decoding the VP8 Shell
Converting WebP back to PNG requires a full mathematical decompression. The algorithm must first undo the "Predictive Blocks" (where WebP guessed pixels based on neighbors) and reconstruct the "Residuals" to get back to the raw RGB and Alpha data. This "Reconstruction math" is what ensures that your final PNG is as sharp as the original file.

### 1.2 The PNG Reconstruction: Building the IDAT
Once the pixels are raw, they must be formatted into PNG-compliant "Scanlines" and filtered using the DEFLATE algorithm. Our tool bridges these fundamental categories, allowing you to instantly generate high-fidelity technical restores that are accurate, professional, and premium, protecting your project's visual integrity.

## 2. A Deep Dive into the Evolution of Computational Imaging

The journey from the "Universal" to the "Optimized" and back again has defined the history of the internet.

### 2.1 The PNG Heritage (1996)
PNG was created to solve the "GIF Patent Scandal," providing a free, open-source way to store high-quality images with transparency. For twenty years, it was the undisputed king of the lossless web, defined by its "IDAT Construction" that made it readable by every piece of software on Earth.

### 2.2 The WebP Transformation (2010 onwards)
As mobile internet became the dominant way humans consumed data, PNG's heavy weight became a liability. Google released WebP to shrink the internet, essentially "Wrapping" the pixel data in a more efficient mathematical skin.

### 2.3 The Compatibility Paradox
Today, we live in a world where "Web-Native" (WebP) and "Edit-Native" (PNG) must coexist. We use WebP-to-PNG conversion as a "Primary Verification" layer for ensuring our assets are "Production-Ready" for every possible software suite, from high-end video editors to local office printers.

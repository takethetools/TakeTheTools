UTM parameters are tags you add to URLs to track where your website traffic comes from. Combined with Google Analytics, they provide deep insight into which marketing channels and campaigns drive results.

## What Are UTM Parameters?

UTM (Urchin Tracking Module) parameters are query string variables appended to your URLs:

- **utm_source**: The traffic source (e.g., `google`, `newsletter`, `twitter`).
- **utm_medium**: The marketing medium (e.g., `cpc`, `email`, `social`).
- **utm_campaign**: Your specific campaign name.
- **utm_term**: The paid keyword (for PPC campaigns).
- **utm_content**: Differentiates similar content (e.g., A/B test variants).

## Example UTM URL

```
https://example.com/landing?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=spring-sale&utm_content=banner-v1
```

## How to Build UTM URLs

1. Open the [UTM Builder](/tools/utm-builder).
2. Enter your destination URL.
3. Fill in the UTM parameters.
4. Copy your generated tracking URL.
5. Shorten it with a URL shortener if needed.

## UTM Best Practices

- **Use lowercase consistently**: `Email` and `email` are tracked separately.
- **Avoid spaces**: Use hyphens or underscores instead.
- **Be consistent**: Document your UTM naming convention for the team.
- **Don't use UTMs on internal links**: Only for incoming external traffic.

Track all your campaigns in Google Analytics under **Acquisition → Campaigns**.
